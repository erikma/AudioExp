Results analysis: Here we vary the shape and size of conv2's kernels, while running through various combinations of number of conv1 filters and number of conv2 filters.

Generally high numbers of conv1 filters show good results - the max at 160 seems to produce a fair number of low losses on test data. yet it's not universal.
The number of conv2 filters does not seem to correlate well - even with 160 conv1 filters the results vary significantly.
Conv2 kernel size and shape produces some interesting results when the kernels are the width of the conv2 space (maxpooled into 6 columns) by 3 or 4 rows.
Yet the previous default 5x5 holds its own fairly well as well.

It seems like 3x6 or 5x5 can do best. Given the historical use of 5x5 so far it seems best to lock that in for now. Conv1 count of 32*categories needs also to be locked in (and maybe higher counts tested for completeness).
As for number of conv2 filters, 16*categories seems like a fair middle ground.
